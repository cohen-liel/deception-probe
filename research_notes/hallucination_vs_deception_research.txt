HALLUCINATION VS DECEPTION RESEARCH - MARCH 2026
=================================================

KEY FINDINGS FROM LATEST RESEARCH:

1. HALLUCINATION VS DECEPTION (ICML 2026)
Paper: "Disentangling Deception and Hallucination Failures in LLMs"
- Hallucination = knowledge doesn't exist in model (irreversible absence)
- Deception = knowledge exists but is suppressed (sparse, distributed suppression)
- Linear Probes: 81% accuracy distinguishing deception from hallucination
- Linear Probes: 92% accuracy distinguishing hallucination from correct answer
- Activation Steering: Can reverse deception (30-50%) but NOT hallucination
- KEY INSIGHT: The information IS there for deception, just suppressed

2. HALLUCINATION DETECTION FROM HIDDEN STATES
Paper: "INSIDE: LLMs' Internal States Retain the Power of Hallucination Detection"
- Middle layers are key (layers 15-21 in 36-layer models)
- Glass ceiling: ~83-84% accuracy, AUC 91% for hallucination detection
- Early layers = syntax, Late layers = generation, Middle layers = semantics

3. OMISSION DETECTION (March 2026)
Paper: "Probing the Limits of the Lie Detector Approach to LLM Deception"
Author: Tom-Felix Berger, Bochum University
- Truth Probes: 83.5% on explicit lies, BUT 47-57% on omission (near chance!)
- Solution: Train on DIALOGUE data (not single sentences) → +15-25% improvement
- Also: Train on "second-order beliefs" (what model thinks USER will believe)

4. CENSORSHIP AS NATURAL TESTBED (March 2026, Harvard + Anthropic)
Paper: "Censored LLMs as a Natural Testbed for Secret Knowledge Elicitation"
- Chinese models (Qwen, DeepSeek) censored on Tiananmen = natural spontaneous lies
- Probes trained on instructed deception → AUROC 0.79-0.82 on censorship lies!
- BUT: Out-of-distribution (TruthfulQA) → drops to 0.53-0.61
- Generalization problem confirmed

IMPLICATIONS FOR DECEPTIONPROBE:
1. Our probe SHOULD be able to distinguish hallucination from deception
2. Omission detection is the hardest problem - dialogue training helps
3. Censored models are a great testbed for spontaneous deception
4. Generalization across domains remains the key challenge
